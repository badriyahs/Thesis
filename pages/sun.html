The Sun

The sun, a celestial body that has guided human perception of time, holds countless meanings across temporal, spiritual, and cultural contexts. This page explores its diverse representations through specific words, each tied to a temporal or spatial connotation.

1. Afala

Definition: Afala means to bow and set. It describes the elongation of the last stretch of heavy time during sunset.

Temporal and Spatial Connotation: This word represents the final moments of the day as the sun sets. It emphasizes the heaviness and length of time at the end of the day.

Notes: Afala symbolizes the sun's transition into the horizon, marking the conclusion of the day with a sense of gravity and finality.

42. La’ab

Definition: La’ab translates to "saliva," but in Arabic, it lacks the negative connotation it might carry in English. Instead, it refers to liquid secretion, often from the mouth. Its meaning shifts based on context, such as lu’ab alnahal ("saliva of the bees") referring to honey, or lu’ab aljarad ("saliva of the insect") referring to poison. In relation to the sun, lu’ab alshams ("saliva of the sun") describes a ray resembling a thread, spilling and pooling.

Temporal and Spatial Connotation: This term is most often used to describe the sun at noon when its rays are most pronounced, creating thread-like visuals. It also refers to the intense heat when the air is still, with rays becoming visibly distinct. It can evoke visual phenomena such as cobweb-like distortions caused by extreme heat, though it is distinct from a mirage, which is associated with the ground, not the sun.
