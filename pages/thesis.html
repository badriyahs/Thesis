Thesis overview

This project stems from a deep exploration of the temporal frameworks that govern our lives. How can we move beyond rigid, universalized notions of time and reclaim our autonomy over how we experience it?

What is "Resistant Temporalities"?

"Resistant Temporalities" is a project that explores alternative ways of understanding and experiencing time ways of understanding and experiencing time. It critiques the linear, imperial, and politicized nature of time and offers ways to reclaim it through connection with nature and ancient time-keeping methods.

What is the purpose of the maps in this project?

The maps in the project are a visual representation of the interconnectedness of time, space, and nature. They challenge the conventional use of maps as mere geographical representations and propose an alternative way to understand our place in the world through time.

How does the project challenge the idea of time?

The project challenges the conventional understanding of time as something linear and imperial by proposing a more fluid, cyclical, and natural approach to time, rooted in ancient ways of perceiving time through the movements of the sun, stars, and natural phenomena.

Research Goals

The research underpinning this thesis investigates:

  • Historical shifts in the concept of time.
  • The psychological and societal impacts of time standardization.
  • Innovative methods for creating alternative temporal systems.
