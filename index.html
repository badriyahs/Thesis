resistant temporalities


as someone who has lived outside of clock time, there is a temporal and spatial experience that we are completely blind to, and we've become dependent on a dictated time, without any understanding of how we fit in it. this project attempts to rehabilitate our innate sense of time, the one we extract from our surroundings, instead of the one blinking on your screen.


here you will find ways to become autonomous in your time and space.

this project begins with a background in how , here is the background about how our time slowly came to be. then, explore the background of this project here, along with some essays that highlight the framework of this project. finally, here are the maps of the sun, clouds, moon & stars, and time itself.

how this project is structured

this project begins with this website, a space dedicated to unraveling the politics and background of time perception. it invites you to reflect on how time is shaped and standardized across different cultures and how it governs our daily lives.


then, we explore the concept of open-source time. how can we access time outside of rigid frameworks? what tools and perspectives can help us understand time more intimately and fluidly? this exploration will empower you to think about time as something personal, rather than dictated.
